1. ResearchGate Discussion Post
==========================================
접속: https://www.researchgate.net → Q&A → Ask a question 또는 Topic Discussion
방법: 관련 토픽 (Statistics, Quantitative Methods, APA Style 등)에서 Discussion 작성

[TITLE]
Free tool for automatic APA 7th edition statistical tables -- looking for feedback from researchers

[BODY]
Dear colleagues,

I'd like to share a free tool I developed that may be useful for researchers who regularly report statistical results in APA format.

StatMate (https://statmate.org) is a web-based statistics calculator that performs 20 common analyses and automatically generates APA 7th edition formatted results tables. The tool includes:

- T-tests (independent, paired, one-sample), ANOVA (one-way, two-way, repeated measures), chi-square, Fisher's exact, correlation, regression (simple, multiple, logistic), nonparametric tests (Mann-Whitney U, Wilcoxon, Kruskal-Wallis, Friedman), factor analysis (EFA), and Cronbach's alpha
- Effect sizes (Cohen's d, eta-squared, partial eta-squared, Cramer's V, etc.) reported by default
- Built-in assumption checks (Shapiro-Wilk for normality, Levene's for homogeneity of variance)
- Confidence intervals where applicable
- One-click export to Word (.docx) or PDF

All computations run client-side in the browser, meaning your data never leaves your computer -- an important consideration for researchers working with sensitive data.

The underlying calculations have been cross-validated against R 4.3 for accuracy. Validation results are available at https://statmate.org/validation.

The tool is free for all 20 calculators and PDF export. A Pro tier ($5.99/month) adds AI-powered interpretation of results and Word export.

I would greatly appreciate feedback from this community, particularly regarding:
1. Numerical accuracy for any edge cases you may encounter
2. Additional tests or features that would be most useful in your workflow
3. Any deviations from APA 7th edition formatting standards

Thank you for your time.

Best regards
